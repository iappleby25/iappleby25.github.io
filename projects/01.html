BlueBikes Dashboard

This dashboard uses the 2019 data from BlueBikes, a Boston-based bike rental service which is providing more than 3.5 million rides per year as of 2023. This project was completed in my Storytelling with Data course at Babson College in Fall 2024. The description for the project was as follows:

Imagine you've applied to a job on Blue Bike's business intelligence team. After the numerous and mind-numbing screening interviews, they've finally given you a practicum: take a year's worth of Blue Bike data (dictionary here) and tell a story. A little taken aback by the entirely open-ended nature of the assignment, you ask whether they might give a little more guidance about what types of insights they're looking for. The hiring manger responds that a big success factor in the role you've applied for is the ability to be a self-starter -- they're much less worried about you asking exactly the business questions they themselves would choose, and instead much more interested in whether you can chart an investigative agenda and see it through to completion. So, then. You're on your own.

Given the open nature of the assignment, I focused on what seemed to be the most interesting element of the data - the start and end stations. This led me to the final dashboard above. I believe that, in line with what the assignment was looking for, the dashboard stands on its own.
